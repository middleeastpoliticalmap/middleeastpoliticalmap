History of the Indiana-Illinois State Line

The Indiana-Illinois state line was officially established in 1809, when the Indiana Territory was created. At that time, the boundary between Indiana and Illinois was the Wabash River. However, in 1818, the boundary was shifted to its current location, which is a straight line running north-south, 10 miles west of the Wabash River.
