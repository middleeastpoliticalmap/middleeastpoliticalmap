Mexican West Coast Map

October 25, 2022 admin 0 Comments

Introduction

Mexico’s West Coast is known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, there’s always something new to discover on the West Coast. In this article, we’ll be exploring the best destinations to visit on the Mexican West Coast in 2023.

1. Puerto Vallarta

Puerto Vallarta is one of the most popular tourist destinations on the West Coast. This charming coastal city has everything you could want in a vacation spot – beautiful beaches, delicious food, and a vibrant nightlife. One of the highlights of Puerto Vallarta is the Malecon, a boardwalk that runs along the beach and is lined with restaurants, bars, and shops.

2. Sayulita

If you’re looking for a more laid-back beach town, Sayulita is the perfect destination. This small surf town is known for its bohemian vibe and stunning beaches. Surfing is a popular activity here, and there are plenty of surf schools for beginners. The town also has plenty of restaurants and shops selling handmade goods.

3. Cabo San Lucas

Cabo San Lucas is a popular destination for those looking for a more luxurious vacation. This resort town has plenty of upscale hotels, restaurants, and shops. One of the highlights of Cabo San Lucas is El Arco, a natural rock formation that juts out into the sea. Boat tours are available for those who want to get a closer look.

4. Mazatlan

Mazatlan is a historic port city that has a rich cultural heritage. The city has a beautiful old town, or centro historico, that is home to many historic buildings and monuments. Mazatlan is also known for its seafood, and there are plenty of restaurants serving fresh catches of the day.

5. La Paz

La Paz is a peaceful and picturesque city located on the Sea of Cortez. This destination is perfect for those who want to get away from the crowds and enjoy some peace and quiet. The city has a beautiful malecon that is perfect for a sunset stroll, and there are plenty of beaches for swimming and snorkeling.

6. Guadalajara

While not located on the coast, Guadalajara is still worth a visit for those who want to experience Mexico’s vibrant culture. This city is known for its mariachi music, tequila, and colonial architecture. There are plenty of museums, markets, and restaurants to explore in Guadalajara.

7. Ensenada

Ensenada is a coastal city located in Baja California. This destination is known for its wine country and delicious seafood. There are plenty of wineries to visit in the surrounding area, and the city itself has plenty of restaurants serving up fresh catches of the day.

8. Puerto Escondido

Puerto Escondido is a laid-back surf town located on the Pacific Coast. This destination is perfect for those who want to spend their vacation relaxing on the beach. The town has plenty of surf schools, restaurants, and shops.

9. Los Cabos

Los Cabos is a region located at the southern tip of the Baja California peninsula. This area is known for its luxury resorts, golf courses, and beautiful beaches. There are plenty of activities to keep you busy in Los Cabos, including snorkeling, diving, and fishing.

10. Zihuatanejo

Zihuatanejo is a small fishing village located on the Pacific Coast. This destination is perfect for those who want to experience Mexico’s authentic culture and cuisine. The town has plenty of seafood restaurants, and there are plenty of beaches to explore in the surrounding area.

Conclusion

The Mexican West Coast has something for everyone, from luxurious resorts to laid-back surf towns. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, there’s a destination on the West Coast that will suit your needs. So start planning your trip to Mexico’s beautiful West Coast in 2023!
