The National Capital Region

The National Capital Region (NCR) of the Philippines is a highly urbanized region that encompasses the country’s capital, Manila, as well as 15 other cities and one municipality. NCR is often referred to as Metro Manila, and it is the most populous region in the country.

The Location of NCR

NCR is located on the island of Luzon, which is the largest island in the Philippines. The region is situated on the west coast of Luzon and is bordered by Manila Bay to the west. NCR is located on the northern part of the island and is surrounded by the provinces of Bulacan and Rizal to the north, Cavite to the south, and Laguna to the southeast.

Size and Population

NCR covers an area of approximately 619.57 square kilometers and has a population of more than 12 million people. The region’s population density is one of the highest in the world, with more than 19,000 people per square kilometer.

The Cities and Municipality of NCR

NCR is composed of the following cities: Caloocan, Las Piñas, Makati, Malabon, Mandaluyong, Manila, Marikina, Muntinlupa, Navotas, Parañaque, Pasay, Pasig, Quezon City, San Juan, Taguig, and Valenzuela. The municipality of Pateros is also part of NCR.

The Capital of the Philippines

Manila, which is located in the southern part of NCR, is the capital of the Philippines. It is the center of government, education, and commerce in the country. Manila is also known for its historical landmarks, such as the walled city of Intramuros and the Rizal Park.

The Business Center of the Philippines

Makati, which is located in the center of NCR, is the country’s financial and business center. It is home to the headquarters of many local and international companies, as well as the Philippine Stock Exchange. Makati is also known for its upscale shopping malls and restaurants.

The Educational Hub of the Philippines

Quezon City, which is located in the northern part of NCR, is the country’s educational hub. It is home to many of the country’s top universities, such as the University of the Philippines and Ateneo de Manila University. Quezon City is also known for its cultural and entertainment attractions, such as the Quezon Memorial Circle and the Araneta Center.

Conclusion

NCR is a highly urbanized region that is located on the island of Luzon in the Philippines. It is composed of 16 cities and one municipality, including the capital city of Manila. NCR is the country’s most populous region and is known for its historical landmarks, financial and business center, and educational hub.
