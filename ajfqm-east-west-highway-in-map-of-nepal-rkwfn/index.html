East West Highway In Map Of Nepal

September 29, 2022 admin 0 Comments

Introduction

Nepal is a country with diverse topography, culture, and ethnicity. In the past, traveling from the eastern part of Nepal to the western side used to be a challenging task due to the lack of proper roads and transportation facilities. However, the construction of the East-West Highway has brought a significant change in the transportation infrastructure of the country.

The History of East-West Highway

The idea of constructing a highway that connects the eastern and western parts of Nepal was first proposed in the early 1950s. It was only in the 1990s that the construction of the highway began, and it took more than a decade to complete the 1,027-kilometer-long road.

The Route of East-West Highway

The East-West Highway starts from Mechinagar in the eastern part of Nepal and ends at Mahendranagar in the western part of Nepal. The highway passes through major cities like Biratnagar, Janakpur, Narayangarh, Butwal, and Dhangadhi, connecting various districts and towns along the way.

The Importance of East-West Highway

The East-West Highway is one of the most crucial transportation routes in Nepal. It has not only facilitated the movement of goods and people but has also opened up new opportunities for economic growth and development in the regions it passes through. The highway has also played a vital role in promoting tourism in Nepal.

Exploring the East-West Highway

Starting Point: Mechinagar

Mechinagar is a small town in the eastern part of Nepal and the starting point of the East-West Highway. The town is known for its beautiful landscapes, scenic views of the Himalayas, and the Kankai River.

Biratnagar: The Industrial Hub

Biratnagar is the second-largest city in Nepal and the industrial hub of the country. The city is known for its large-scale industries, including jute and textile factories. It is also the gateway to the Koshi Tappu Wildlife Reserve, a popular destination for birdwatchers.

Janakpur: The Holy City

Janakpur is a city in the southern part of Nepal and is considered one of the holiest cities in the country. The city has many temples, including the Janaki Temple, which is dedicated to the goddess Sita. Janakpur is also known for its Mithila art, a traditional art form that originated in the region.

Narayangarh: The Commercial Hub

Narayangarh is a city in the central part of Nepal and the commercial hub of the region. The city is known for its bustling markets, shopping centers, and restaurants. It is also the gateway to the Chitwan National Park, one of the most popular tourist destinations in Nepal.

Butwal: The Gateway to the West

Butwal is a city in the western part of Nepal and the gateway to the western regions of the country. The city is known for its beautiful landscapes, including the Tinau River and the Rani Mahal Palace. Butwal is also the center of the Rupandehi district, which is known for its agricultural products.

Dhangadhi: The Far West

Dhangadhi is a city in the far-western part of Nepal and the last major city on the East-West Highway. The city is known for its unique cultural heritage, including the Tharu culture and the Kailali district. Dhangadhi is also the gateway to the Shuklaphanta National Park, a popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

Conclusion

The East-West Highway has transformed the transportation infrastructure of Nepal, connecting various regions of the country and opening up new opportunities for economic growth and development. The highway has also played a vital role in promoting tourism in Nepal, showcasing the country’s diverse topography, culture, and ethnicity. Exploring the East-West Highway is an adventure worth taking, offering a glimpse into the heart of Nepal.
