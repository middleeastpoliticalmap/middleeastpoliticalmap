Physical Map Of India With Mountains Detail

December 24, 2022 admin 0 Comments

The Geography of India

India is a vast country, covering an area of 3.3 million square kilometers. It is the seventh-largest country in the world and the second-most populous country. India is known for its diverse culture, heritage, and traditions. The country is surrounded by the Bay of Bengal in the east, the Arabian Sea in the west, and the Indian Ocean to the south. The Himalayan mountain range to the north separates India from the rest of Asia.

Physical Map of India

The physical map of India shows the different landforms and topography of the country. The map depicts the mountain ranges, plateaus, plains, rivers, and other physical features of India. The map also highlights the location of major cities and towns, national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and other important landmarks.

The Mountains of India

The Himalayan mountain range is the most prominent feature of the physical map of India. The range stretches over 2,400 kilometers, covering five states in India. The Himalayas are home to the highest peak in the world, Mount Everest, which is located on the border of Nepal and Tibet. Apart from the Himalayas, the Western Ghats and the Eastern Ghats are the other two prominent mountain ranges in India. The Western Ghats are located along the western coast of India, covering six states. The Eastern Ghats run parallel to the eastern coast of India, covering five states.

The Importance of Mountains

Mountains play a crucial role in the ecology and economy of India. The Himalayan mountain range acts as a natural barrier, protecting India from the cold winds of Central Asia. The mountain ranges also act as water towers, providing water to the plains and low-lying areas. The Western Ghats and the Eastern Ghats are home to several endemic species of flora and fauna, making them a biodiversity hotspot.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the physical map of India with mountains detail is an important tool for understanding the geography and topography of the country. The mountains of India are not only breathtakingly beautiful but also play a significant role in the ecology and economy of the country. By studying the physical map of India, one can gain a better appreciation of the diverse landscape of the country.
