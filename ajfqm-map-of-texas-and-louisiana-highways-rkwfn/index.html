Map Of Texas And Louisiana Highways

August 14, 2022 admin 0 Comments

Introduction

Are you planning to travel through Texas or Louisiana in the coming year? If so, you’ll want to have a good understanding of the highways and roads that connect these two states. In this article, we’ll take a closer look at the map of Texas and Louisiana highways in 2023. From major interstates to scenic byways, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your next road trip.

Interstates

When it comes to highways in Texas and Louisiana, the interstates are the backbone of the transportation system. In Texas, three major interstates run east to west: I-10, I-20, and I-30. In Louisiana, I-10 runs east to west, while I-20 and I-49 run north to south. These interstates provide quick and efficient routes for both commercial trucks and personal vehicles.

I-10

I-10 is the southernmost interstate in Texas and Louisiana, running from El Paso, Texas to Jacksonville, Florida. In Texas, I-10 passes through major cities such as Houston and San Antonio, while in Louisiana it runs through Lake Charles and Baton Rouge. For those looking to travel quickly between the two states, I-10 is the best option.

I-20

I-20 runs from West Texas to South Carolina, passing through major cities such as Dallas, Shreveport, and Atlanta. In Texas, it runs through the heart of the state, connecting major cities such as Midland and Odessa with Dallas and Fort Worth. In Louisiana, it runs through the northern part of the state, connecting Shreveport with Monroe and Vicksburg, Mississippi.

I-30

I-30 runs from Fort Worth to Little Rock, Arkansas, passing through major cities such as Dallas and Texarkana. In Texas, it connects the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex with the eastern part of the state, providing a quick route to Arkansas and beyond. In Louisiana, it runs through the northern part of the state, connecting Shreveport with Bossier City and beyond.

US Highways

In addition to the interstates, there are also several US highways that connect Texas and Louisiana. These highways offer a more scenic route and often pass through smaller towns and cities.

US-59

US-59 runs from Laredo, Texas to the Canadian border, passing through major cities such as Houston and Texarkana. In Texas, it runs parallel to I-45, connecting Houston with Lufkin and beyond. In Louisiana, it runs through the western part of the state, passing through cities such as Shreveport and Natchitoches.

US-190

US-190 runs from I-10 in Texas to I-12 in Louisiana, passing through major cities such as Baton Rouge and Killeen. In Texas, it runs through the central part of the state, connecting cities such as Bryan and College Station with Austin and beyond. In Louisiana, it runs through the southern part of the state, passing through towns and cities such as Opelousas and Hammond.

State Highways

Finally, there are also several state highways that connect Texas and Louisiana. These highways often provide access to smaller towns and attractions that may not be accessible via the interstates or US highways.

TX-63

TX-63 runs from Jasper, Texas to the Louisiana state line, connecting towns such as Burkeville and Hemphill. In Louisiana, it becomes LA-8 and runs through towns such as Leesville and Anacoco.

LA-12

LA-12 runs from the Texas state line to the Mississippi state line, passing through towns such as DeQuincy and Leesville. It provides access to attractions such as Toledo Bend Reservoir and the Kisatchie National Forest.

Conclusion

Whether you’re traveling for business or pleasure, understanding the map of Texas and Louisiana highways is essential. From the major interstates to the scenic byways, there are plenty of routes to choose from when planning your next road trip. So grab your map, plot your course, and get ready for an adventure in the heart of the South.
