Introduction

If you’re planning on visiting or moving to Dekalb County, Indiana, having a map of the area is essential. Not only will it help you navigate around the county, but it will also help you discover all the hidden gems that it has to offer.

History of Dekalb County

Dekalb County was founded in 1835 and named after Johann von Robais, Baron de Kalb, a German officer who fought for the Continental Army during the American Revolutionary War. The county is located in northeastern Indiana and is home to several small towns and cities.

Geography and Climate

Dekalb County covers an area of 364 square miles and is bordered by Steuben, Noble, Allen, and Whitley counties. The county is part of the Great Lakes region and experiences a humid continental climate with four distinct seasons.

Points of Interest

Dekalb County is home to several points of interest that are worth visiting, including the Auburn Cord Duesenberg Automobile Museum, the National Military History Center, and the Wild Winds Buffalo Preserve. The county also has several parks and recreational areas, including the Auburn-Fairview Trail and the Cedar Creek Golf Club.

Transportation

Dekalb County is located near several major highways, including Interstate 69 and State Road 8, making it easy to get around the area. The county also has several airports nearby, including the Fort Wayne International Airport and the DeKalb County Airport.

Education

Dekalb County is home to several school districts, including the Dekalb County Central United School District and the Garrett-Keyser-Butler Community School District. The county also has several higher education institutions nearby, including Indiana University-Purdue University Fort Wayne and Trine University.

Real Estate

If you’re thinking of moving to Dekalb County, there are several real estate options available, including single-family homes, townhouses, and apartments. The county has a relatively low cost of living compared to other areas in the state, making it an attractive place to live.

Conclusion

Dekalb County, Indiana, is a beautiful area with lots to offer. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or just want to enjoy a quiet life, Dekalb County has something for everyone. Make sure to get your map of the area before you visit, so you can make the most of your time there.
