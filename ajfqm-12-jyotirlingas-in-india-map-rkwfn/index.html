12 Jyotirlingas In India Map

Introduction

India is a land full of spirituality and religion. One of the most significant aspects of Hinduism is the belief in Jyotirlingas, which are considered the holiest shrines of Lord Shiva. There are 12 Jyotirlingas spread across India, each with a unique history and significance. In this article, we will explore the 12 Jyotirlingas in India map.

Location of the 12 Jyotirlingas

The 12 Jyotirlingas are located in different parts of India. Some of them are situated in the mountains, while others are located in the plains. The following is the list of the 12 Jyotirlingas and their locations: 1. Somnath Jyotirlinga, Gujarat 2. Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga, Andhra Pradesh 3. Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga, Madhya Pradesh 4. Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga, Madhya Pradesh 5. Kedarnath Jyotirlinga, Uttarakhand 6. Bhimashankar Jyotirlinga, Maharashtra 7. Viswanath Jyotirlinga, Uttar Pradesh 8. Triambakeshwar Jyotirlinga, Maharashtra 9. Vaidyanath Jyotirlinga, Jharkhand 10. Nageshwar Jyotirlinga, Gujarat 11. Rameshwaram Jyotirlinga, Tamil Nadu 12. Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga, Maharashtra

Significance of the 12 Jyotirlingas

The 12 Jyotirlingas are considered the holiest shrines of Lord Shiva. It is believed that visiting these shrines and offering prayers to Lord Shiva can bring immense blessings and prosperity. Each Jyotirlinga has its own unique significance and history.

Somnath Jyotirlinga

Somnath Jyotirlinga is located in Gujarat and is considered the first among the 12 Jyotirlingas. It is believed that Lord Shiva himself established this shrine. The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt several times throughout history.

Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga

Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga is located in Andhra Pradesh and is associated with the story of Lord Shiva and Parvati. It is believed that Parvati did penance here to win Lord Shiva’s hand in marriage.

Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga

Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga is located in Madhya Pradesh and is considered one of the most powerful Jyotirlingas. The temple is known for its unique architecture and is believed to be the only Jyotirlinga where Lord Shiva is worshipped in the form of a lingam facing south.

Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga

Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga is also located in Madhya Pradesh and is associated with the story of the Vindhya mountains. It is believed that Lord Shiva appeared here to resolve a dispute between the Vindhya mountains and the river Narmada.

Kedarnath Jyotirlinga

Kedarnath Jyotirlinga is located in Uttarakhand and is situated at an altitude of over 11,000 feet. It is believed that the temple was built by the Pandavas during the Mahabharata era.

Bhimashankar Jyotirlinga

Bhimashankar Jyotirlinga is located in Maharashtra and is associated with the story of Lord Shiva and Parvati. It is believed that Parvati did penance here to win Lord Shiva’s hand in marriage.

Viswanath Jyotirlinga

Viswanath Jyotirlinga is located in Uttar Pradesh and is considered one of the most sacred Jyotirlingas. The temple is known for its unique architecture and is believed to have been built by the Queen of Indore.

Triambakeshwar Jyotirlinga

Triambakeshwar Jyotirlinga is located in Maharashtra and is associated with the story of Gautama Rishi. It is believed that Gautama Rishi did penance here to please Lord Shiva and was granted a boon.

Vaidyanath Jyotirlinga

Vaidyanath Jyotirlinga is located in Jharkhand and is associated with the story of Ravana. It is believed that Ravana did penance here to please Lord Shiva and was granted a boon.

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga is located in Gujarat and is associated with the story of demon Daruka. It is believed that Lord Shiva appeared here to destroy the demon and bless his devotees.

Rameshwaram Jyotirlinga

Rameshwaram Jyotirlinga is located in Tamil Nadu and is associated with the story of Lord Rama. It is believed that Lord Rama worshipped Lord Shiva here before embarking on his journey to Lanka to rescue Sita.

Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga

Grishneshwar Jyotirlinga is located in Maharashtra and is associated with the story of a woman named Kusuma. It is believed that Kusuma did penance here to please Lord Shiva and was granted a boon.

Conclusion

The 12 Jyotirlingas in India map are a symbol of the rich cultural heritage of India. These shrines attract devotees from all over the world and offer a unique insight into the spirituality and religion of India. A pilgrimage to these holy shrines is considered a once-in-a-lifetime experience and can bring immense blessings and prosperity.
