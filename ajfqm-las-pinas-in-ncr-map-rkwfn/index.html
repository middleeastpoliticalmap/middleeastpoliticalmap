Las Pinas In Ncr Map

September 20, 2022 admin 0 Comments

Introduction

Located in the southern part of Metro Manila, Las Pinas is a highly urbanized city that boasts of its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, and modern attractions. Despite being one of the smallest cities in the National Capital Region (NCR), Las Pinas is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of old and new, making it a must-visit destination for tourists and locals alike.

Getting There

Las Pinas is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. If you’re coming from Manila, you can take the LRT-1 or MRT-3 and get off at the Baclaran station. From there, you can ride a jeepney or a bus that will take you to Las Pinas. You can also opt to take a taxi or a Grab car for a more convenient and hassle-free travel.

Historical Attractions

One of the most popular landmarks in Las Pinas is the Bamboo Organ, which is considered as one of the world’s finest pipe organs. This 19th-century instrument is made entirely of bamboo and is located inside the St. Joseph Parish Church. Another must-visit historical site is the Sarao Motors factory, which is known for producing the iconic Philippine jeepney.

Natural Wonders

Las Pinas is home to several natural wonders that are worth exploring. One of these is the Las Pinas-Paranaque Critical Habitat and Ecotourism Area, which is a protected wetland that serves as a sanctuary for various bird species. Another must-visit natural attraction is the Zapote River, which is a popular spot for kayaking and other water activities.

Modern Attractions

Aside from its historical and natural wonders, Las Pinas also offers modern attractions that cater to different interests. For thrill-seekers, there’s the Dreamworld Theme Park, which has a variety of rides and attractions for all ages. There’s also the SM Southmall, which is a shopping center that features various local and international brands.

Food Scene

Las Pinas is a foodie’s paradise, with a wide range of dining options that cater to every taste and budget. Some of the must-try dishes include the crispy pata and lechon kawali at Aling Nene’s, the seafood feast at Dampa Seafood Palutuan, and the homemade ice cream at Arce Dairy.

Accommodation

There are plenty of accommodation options in Las Pinas, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the highly recommended places to stay include the Eurotel Las Pinas, the RedDoorz Premium near SM Southmall, and the Vivere Hotel and Resorts.

Events and Festivals

Las Pinas is also known for its vibrant and colorful festivals, which showcase the city’s rich culture and traditions. One of the most popular events is the Bamboo Organ Festival, which is held every February and features various musical performances. There’s also the Las Pinas Food Festival, which highlights the city’s unique culinary offerings.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural wonders, modern attractions, and vibrant culture, Las Pinas is a destination that has something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, a foodie, or a thrill-seeker, Las Pinas is a city that will surprise and delight you. So, the next time you plan your itinerary, make sure to include Las Pinas in your list and discover why it’s a hidden gem in the south of Metro Manila.
