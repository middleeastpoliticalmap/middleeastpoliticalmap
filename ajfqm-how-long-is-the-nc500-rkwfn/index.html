How Long Is The Nc500

February 17, 2023 admin 0 Comments

Introduction

The North Coast 500, also known as the NC500, is a popular scenic route in Scotland that offers breathtaking views of the country’s stunning landscape. This 500-mile route takes you through some of the most picturesque villages, rugged coastlines, and majestic mountain ranges in Scotland. In this article, we will answer the question that many travelers ask: How long is the NC500?

The NC500 Route

The NC500 route is a circular road trip that starts and ends at Inverness Castle. The route takes you through the Scottish Highlands, where you can explore the rugged coastline, dramatic mountains, and pristine lochs. You will also get a chance to visit some of Scotland’s most charming villages, including Ullapool, Durness, and John O’Groats.

Driving Time

The total driving time for the NC500 is around 12-14 hours, depending on how many stops you make along the way. You can complete the route in a day, but we recommend taking at least a week to fully appreciate the beauty of the Scottish Highlands.

Distance

The North Coast 500 is a 500-mile route that takes you through some of the most remote and untouched parts of Scotland. The route covers a distance of 516 miles (830 km) and offers plenty of opportunities to explore and discover the beauty of Scotland.

What to See on the NC500

If you’re planning a trip on the NC500, there are plenty of things to see and do along the way. Here are some of the highlights of the route:

Ullapool

Ullapool is a charming fishing village located on the shores of Loch Broom. The village is known for its stunning scenery and excellent seafood restaurants.

John O’Groats

John O’Groats is a small village located on the northeastern tip of Scotland. It is famous for being the northernmost point of the British mainland and is a popular tourist destination.

Durness Beach

Durness Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in Scotland. It’s a great place to relax and soak up the stunning scenery.

When to Go on the NC500

The best time to go on the NC500 is during the summer months (June to August). The weather is mild, and the days are long, which gives you more time to explore the stunning scenery.

Winter

Winter can be a challenging time to travel on the NC500, as the roads can be icy and snow-covered. However, if you’re looking for a winter adventure, the route can be just as beautiful during this time of year.

Conclusion

The North Coast 500 is a 500-mile road trip that offers some of the most breathtaking scenery in Scotland. The route takes you through some of the most remote and untouched parts of the country, and there are plenty of things to see and do along the way. The total driving time for the NC500 is around 12-14 hours, but we recommend taking at least a week to fully appreciate the beauty of the Scottish Highlands. So, pack your bags, hit the road, and enjoy the journey!
