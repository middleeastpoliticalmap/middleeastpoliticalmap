Introduction

History of Queens Borough

Queens Borough was established in 1683 and was named after Catherine of Braganza, the queen consort of King Charles II of England. It was originally part of the province of New York and was under Dutch rule until the British took control in 1664. Today, Queens Borough is a thriving community that is home to over 2.2 million people.
