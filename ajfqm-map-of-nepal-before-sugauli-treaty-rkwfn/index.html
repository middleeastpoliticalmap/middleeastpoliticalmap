The History of Nepal and the Sugauli Treaty

Nepal is a country with a rich history that predates its modern borders. The region was once divided into small principalities that were united under the Gorkha dynasty in the 18th century. The Gorkha kingdom expanded its territory and became Nepal, a nation that was ruled by a monarchy until 2008. The Sugauli Treaty was signed in 1816 between the British East India Company and Nepal. The treaty ended the Anglo-Nepalese War, which lasted from 1814 to 1816. The treaty established Nepal’s current borders and ceded some territories to the British, including parts of present-day India.

The northern border of Nepal before the treaty was the Kali River, which separates Nepal from present-day Tibet. The region north of the Kali River was known as Tibet’s Western Region and was a part of Nepal’s territory until the treaty.
